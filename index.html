How I am spending my days right now

I work as a front end developer at Gene Commerce that are based in Brighton, UK.

We build and maintain e-commerce websites using the platform Magento - I became a certified Magento Front-End Developer in November 2019.

How much I love a good community

I started out as a codebar student myself in 2016, and thanks to the people I met along the way I was able to get a job in the industry. I love to give back to the community and my goal is to make every person I meet to be feel as supported and welcomed as I once was. As I started teaching and mentoring others, I realised how much I enjoy it - so it's really a win-win situation.

Code First Girls - Instructor & Fellow

Code First Girls are dedicated to transforming tech by providing the skills, space and inspiration for women to become kickass developers and future leaders. Their active community of coders, trainers and coaches is one of the largest in the UK, facilitating women to break into and excel within the industry.

In 2020, I was accepted and completed the Code First Girls Fellowship, a 2-month programme dedicated to developing the next generation of tech leaders through immersive teaching and coaching opportunities.

As a part of this program, I taught a Introduction to Web Development class over 8 weeks, once a week. We were 3 instructors and I lead 3 classes on my own. We covered topics from HTML, CSS, JavaScript, frameworks and GitHub to help the students build their own website at the end of the course!

I was also an instructor for Introduction to Web Development in 2019 at Sussex University in Brighton.

codebar - Organiser and coach

codebar is a UK charity that runs free, weekly workshops to help underrepresented learn coding in a safe and collaborative environment.

I started attending codebar after finishing my web design diploma in 2016. Every Tuesday evening for about 1 year, I would meet new companies and one day codebar was hosted at Gene, I met one of their developers who introduced me to the boss - and I ended up with a junior position!

After starting my job, I wanted to give back to this amazing community. I started coaches new beginners in HTML/CSS and then was asked to help organise the workshops as well. As a organiser, the responsibilites was to find companies to host/sponsor the weekly workshops, pair students with coaches and spread awareness in the Brighton tech community.

I also helped students with job applications, creating connections and being their reference. My favorite thing about codebar was all the amazing people I met and the connections made, both professionally and personally!

In 2019, I stopped volunteering as an codebar organiser to start being an instructor at CFG. I still return as a coach sometimes :)

Where I am from

I grew up in a very rural area called SvenshÃ¶jden in Sweden. It's a quiet place, very few neighbours, no traffic and there is 30 minutes drive to the closest shop. I go back few times a year as my parents still live here.

At Karlstad University, I studied Visual Communication and Design. I always knew I wanted to work with something creative but I wasn't sure what that was. I used to play around with web design a bit in my spare time but didn't realise it was a profession.

Before moving to Brighton in 2015, I lived and worked in Norway for 2 years. In 2012, I also spent 6 months in Richmond, Virginia as a part of an exchange at university.

After 2 years in Norway, I wanted to move abroad again and came across a web design diploma course in Brighton. I decided to go for it, moved to Brighton and this diploma ignited my passion for web development.

The diploma lasted 6 months and during this time, I realised Brighton would be a great place to start my new career. I started to attend codebar, I spent a year building up skills and my portfolio. Then the magic happened - thanks to connections I made from codebar, I got offered a junior position at Gene!
